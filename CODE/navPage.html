2003

ANONYMOUS FIRST ORIGINATES ON 4CHAN

4chan is an imageboard website where posts generally have anonymous authors. The concept of Anonymous began as a decentralized community on 4chan that would act anonymously in a coordinated manner. These coordinated efforts were usually toward a loosely self-agreed goal that was primarily for entertainment purposes (or lulz). In the next 15 years this small and largely immature 4chan community would evolve into an international hactivist group.

SP - an abbreviation of Supressive Person, a term used in Scientology to describe "antisocial personalities." Many who end up leaving the church are labeled as SPs.

2008

PROJECT CHANOLOGY

After a video leaked of Tom Cruise gushing about his total devotion to the doctrines of The Church of Scientology, the Internet and 4chan users in praticular had a field day making memes of the video. However, the church began trying to expunge the video from the Internet; sending legal nastygrams to anyone hosting or sharing it. The anons were so enraged by this attempt at censorship that they set out to inflict as much damage as possible on the church. So began Project Chanology, in which the anons wanted not only the lulz, but also for those lulz to have a real-world effect. In dedicating themselves to that latter goal, Anonymous began to develop a real political consciousness.

2010

OPERATION PAYBACK

After WikiLeak's release of hundreds of thousands of confidential U.S. documents, the three companies began blocking payments to the site for alleged terms-of-service violations. The action struck many anons as a transparent attempt to hobble Julian Assange's whistle-blowing organization. So began Operation Payback, in which anons used illegal DDoS attacks to take down the websites of MasterCard and Visa and briefly slow down PayPal to a crawl. In order to avoid jail time, the anons planned and carried out these attacks using the anonymity provided by the darknet.

2010

ROSS ULBRICT BEGINS DEVELOPEMENT ON SILK ROAD

At the age of 26, Ulbrict - a libretarian idealist from Texas - began buidling the website Silk Road. He envisioned the site as a free market experiment with an emphasis on user anonymity. To achieve this, Ulbrict planned to host the site on the darknet, making communition between users entirely anonymous. Ulbrict designed the site to only handle transactions made in bitcoin, offering a level of anonymity far greater than any other form of currency or credit card. As a result, Silk Road’s existence would be completely outside the scope of government control, allowing users to buy or sell almost anything.

2010

SILK ROAD BECOMES THE BIGGEST DARK WEB BLACK MARKET

Much of this success can be attributed to Silk Road’s trustworthiness. By establishing a rating and feedback system, Silk Road eliminated much of the fear of buying fake or altered products. Additionally, since purchases were occurring on an online platform, Silk Road eliminated the dangers of meeting with nefarious dealers in person. Ross even created a commercial for the site. In essence, Silk Road brought the drug trade into the 21st century.

People should have the right to buy and sell whatever they wanted so long as they aren't hurting anyone

– ROSS ULBRICT

2010

Despite creating terms of service that were more prohibitive than other dark web markets, Ulbricht became unwilling or unable to maintain the standards that he had initially set, and as the site evolved, more and more dangerous products, including heroin, guns, and fentanyl, began to be listed. All these listings were easily accessbile to everyone of all ages, all you needed was a shipping address and a bitcoin account.

2013

AFTER ALMOST A TWO-YEAR LONG INVESTIGATION THE FBI SHUTS DOWN SILK ROAD

Ulbricht was arrested and sentenced to life in prison(more info here conviceted crimes etc)*. As part of their investigation, they determined that over $1.2 billion in sales had occurred involving 150,000 customers and 4000 vendors.
